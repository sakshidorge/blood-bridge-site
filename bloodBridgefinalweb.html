Why Donate?
One Donation, Three Lives
❤️
Save Up to 3 Lives
One blood donation is separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets — each helping a different patient in need.
⚡
Fast & Easy
The actual donation takes only 8–10 minutes. The full process including registration is under 45 minutes.
🔄
Replenishes Quickly
Your body replaces lost fluids in 24 hours. Red blood cells are fully restored within 4–6 weeks.

✅ Eligibility Criteria
  • Age between 18 and 65 years
  • Weight at least 50 kg
  • Hemoglobin ≥ 12.5 g/dL
  • No donation in the last 3 months
  • Pulse between 60–100 bpm
  • Normal blood pressure (120/80)
  • Active infections or fever
  • Recent surgery (within 6 months)
  • Pregnancy or recent childbirth
  • Certain chronic medications
💡
Before You Donate
Drink plenty of water, eat a healthy iron-rich meal, get a good night's sleep, and wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that roll up easily.

Donor Education
Blood Donation Tips
Everything you need to know before, during, and after donating blood safely.
💧
Stay Hydrated
Drink at least 16 oz of water before donating. Hydration helps your veins be accessible and makes donation easier.
🥗
Eat Beforehand
Have a healthy iron-rich meal 2–3 hours before donating. Avoid fatty foods which can affect blood testing results.
😴
Rest Well
Get at least 8 hours of sleep the night before. Being well-rested ensures a smooth donation experience.
🚫
Avoid Alcohol
Don't consume alcohol for at least 24 hours before donating. Alcohol dehydrates and affects hemoglobin levels.
👕
Dress Comfortably
Wear a shirt with sleeves that roll up above the elbow. Comfort reduces anxiety and helps the process go smoothly.
🏃
After Donation
Rest 10–15 minutes, drink juice, avoid strenuous activity for 24 hours, keep the bandage on for at least 4 hours.
🩺
Iron-Rich Diet
Eat spinach, beans, and red meat regularly to maintain hemoglobin levels required for donation (≥12.5 g/dL).
📅
Donation Frequency
Whole blood: every 56 days. Platelets: every 7 days. Plasma: every 28 days. Max 24 platelet donations/year.
🏥
Health Benefits
Regular donation reduces iron overload, may lower heart disease risk, and includes a free mini health check.
